ANCIENT HISTORY


Ancient history refers to the period of human history from the earliest known civilizations to the fall of the Western Roman Empire in AD 476. This period is typically divided into three major eras: the Stone Age (circa 2.5 million years ago to 3000 BCE), the Bronze Age (3000 BCE to 1200 BCE), and the Iron Age (1200 BCE to 476 CE). Stone Age The Stone Age is the earliest period of human history and is characterized by the use of stone tools. It is divided into three sub-periods: the Paleolithic, Mesolithic, and Neolithic. The Paleolithic period (circa 2.5 million years ago to 10,000 BCE) is the longest period of the Stone Age and is characterized by the development of early human species such as Homo habilis, Homo erectus, and Homo sapiens. During this time, humans were primarily nomadic hunters and gatherers. The Mesolithic period (10,000 BCE to 8000 BCE) saw the development of agriculture and the domestication of animals. The Neolithic period (8000 BCE to 3000 BCE) saw the emergence of civilization and the development of permanent settlements, such as Çatalhöyük in Turkey and Jericho in Palestine. Bronze Age The Bronze Age saw the development of metalworking, the use of bronze as a material, and the rise of the first civilizations. The Bronze Age is divided into three periods: the Early Bronze Age (3000 BCE to 2000 BCE), the Middle Bronze Age (2000 BCE to 1600 BCE), and the Late Bronze Age (1600 BCE to 1200 BCE). The Early Bronze Age saw the development of the Sumerian civilization in Mesopotamia, the Indus Valley civilization in South Asia, and the Old Kingdom of Egypt. The Middle Bronze Age saw the rise of the Babylonian empire and the Minoan civilization on the island of Crete. The Late Bronze Age saw the emergence of the Mycenaean civilization in Greece and the Hittite empire in Anatolia. Iron Age The Iron Age saw the widespread use of iron tools and weapons, the development of new technologies, and the rise of new empires. The Iron Age is divided into two periods: the Early Iron Age (1200 BCE to 800 BCE) and the Late Iron Age (800 BCE to 476 CE). The Early Iron Age saw the emergence of the Phoenician civilization in the eastern Mediterranean, the Assyrian empire in Mesopotamia, and the Kingdom of Israel in Palestine. The Late Iron Age saw the rise of the Persian empire, the Greek city-states, the Roman republic, and eventually the Roman empire. Overall, ancient history is a rich and complex field of study that encompasses the origins of human civilization, the development of early technologies, the rise and fall of empires, and the emergence of cultural traditions that continue to shape our world today.
